Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about converting PDF to Word documents.

How does the PDF to Word conversion work?

Our advanced conversion technology analyzes the structure and content of your PDF file and reconstructs it as an editable Word document. We preserve text formatting, tables, images, and layout to the greatest extent possible during the conversion process.

Is there a file size limit for conversion?

You can convert PDF files up to 50MB in size. For larger files, we recommend using our desktop software version which can handle files of any size.

Will my converted document look exactly like the original PDF?

While we strive to maintain the original formatting, some complex layouts may require minor adjustments in Word. Text-heavy documents typically convert with near-perfect accuracy, while documents with complex graphics, columns, or tables may need some reformatting.

Is my document secure when using your converter?

Absolutely. Your files are processed securely and automatically deleted from our servers after conversion. We do not store or share your documents, and all transfers are encrypted for maximum security.

Can I convert scanned PDFs to Word?

Yes, our converter includes OCR (Optical Character Recognition) technology that can extract text from scanned documents. For best results with scanned PDFs, ensure the scan quality is high and the text is clear.
